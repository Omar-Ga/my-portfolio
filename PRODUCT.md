# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, recruiters at tech companies, and freelance clients who may need a senior full-stack engineer or technical co-founder. They arrive with high intent but low patience: within seconds they should feel that this person operates on a distinctly different level from the template-clone crowd. The desired outcome is a reached-out email or DM.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Omar Gamal — full-stack engineer. The site is a piece of creative work in itself: the craft of building it is part of the proof. It exists to convert qualified viewers into contacts. Success looks like: visitor arrives, feels something genuine, and decides Omar is worth reaching out to.

## Brand Personality

Experimental, expressive, unconventional. Not safe. Not template. The portfolio should feel like a genuine piece of creative work — the kind that makes you pause and want to understand how it was made. Tone is confident without being loud, distinctive without being chaotic.

## Anti-references

- Generic developer portfolios — Bootstrap clones, card-grid-of-projects with tech-stack pills.
- Safe corporate-clean — looks like a LinkedIn profile with a domain name.
- The oversaturated dark + purple gradient + floating code-card aesthetic.
- Over-engineered SaaS style — pastel cards, feature grids, hero metric bubbles.
- Warm cream/sand editorial — linen tones, every-design-Twitter serif aesthetic.

## Design Principles

1. **Creative work, not a brochure.** The portfolio is itself the argument. Every section should communicate craft by demonstrating it, not by listing it.
2. **Earned surprise.** Experimental moments should feel purposeful, not gratuitous. Surprise the viewer once per scroll depth; don't spray effects everywhere.
3. **Confidence through restraint.** The unconventional choice is often the more minimal one. Avoid decoration for decoration's sake; let structure and motion do the work.
4. **Show, don't list.** Prefer demonstrating skills through the site itself (animations, interactions, layout choices) over bullet-pointing technologies.
5. **The viewer is not a reader.** Assume the first pass is a 10-second scan. Every section must earn a second look before demanding a first read.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No strict WCAG level mandated. Contrast should be adequate for practical readability, but creative and artistic expression takes precedence over strict compliance on hero and display-level text. Reduced-motion support is non-negotiable (already in place via GSAP + CSS media query).
